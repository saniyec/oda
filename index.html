Note types

Official chart and private supervision kept separate

This separation is an ethical requirement. Oda enforces it visually and in exported PDFs.

Official chart notes
Clinical Note (DAP)
Data, Assessment, Plan — standard for private practice. Brief, clinical, legally defensible.
Risk Assessment
Suicidal ideation, self-harm, protective factors, safety plan — systematic coverage.
Session Summary
Brief narrative of themes, presentation, insights, and next session plan.
Private supervision notes
Process Note
Detailed narrative for supervision — full sentences, silences, affect shifts. Physically separate from the chart.
Countertransference Note
Therapist's own reactions, thoughts, bodily sensations. First person, honest. For supervision only.

Oda was born from a specific frustration. After years across psychiatric inpatient settings, digital mental health platforms, and private practice — in Turkish and English, CBT and psychoanalytic — I kept hitting the same wall: documentation tools built for a different therapist in a different country.

No tool understood that a process note is fundamentally different from a progress note. None knew what a countertransference note was. None worked in Turkish. None took KVKK seriously.

I built Oda because I needed it. Designed by someone who's written hundreds of DAP notes, sat in psychoanalytic supervision, worked with LGBTQIA+ clients in Turkey, and researched at Radboud and Bilkent UMRAM. The clinical knowledge is built in.
